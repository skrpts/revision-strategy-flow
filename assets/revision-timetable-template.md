## Adjustment Triggers

Review and adjust the timetable if any of the following occur:
- [ ] A weekly confidence reassessment shows a topic has not improved after 2+ sessions
- [ ] A topic proves significantly harder or easier than expected
- [ ] Available study time changes due to unexpected commitments
- [ ] A mock exam or practice question set reveals a new weak area
- [ ] You are consistently finishing sessions early (increase difficulty) or running over (reduce scope)

## Usage Instructions

1. Fill in the Header and Exam Schedule first.
2. Complete the Confidence Map Summary using the Topic Confidence Rater.
3. Build the weekly schedule starting from the first week, allocating time based on confidence priorities.
4. Schedule weak topics for at least 4 sessions each, spread across the revision period.
5. Include at least one rest day per week and keep study blocks to 45-90 minutes maximum.
6. Review and adjust every Sunday during the weekly review session.
7. Use the Adjustment Triggers to know when to modify the plan.
